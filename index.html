xCeption Robotics Team

xCeption is an innovative robotics team pushing the boundaries of technology and creativity. We design, build, and program intelligent robots, turning bold ideas into real-world solutions through engineering, teamwork, and passion.
We are a young FTC team from NIS PhMD Astana. Each of us is part of a bigger journey: some write code, some design parts, and others assemble the robot by hand. In this blog, we’ll share the behind-the-scenes of FTC — build stages, testing, failures, and victories. Stay with us — the best is yet to come.

Discover innovation today

Outreach events

Team xCeption conducts outreach events to inspire students, share engineering knowledge, and support sustainable STEM education in our community.

Fab lab NU

To gain access to more advanced resources we visited the FabLab at Nazarbayev University which is part of NURIS several times. Here we had the opportunity to practice with 3D printers, meet Rustam Askaruly, and see the advanced technologies

Mentorship from xCellence

We also sought help and support from more experienced individuals with the senior robotics team at our school xCellence serving as our mentors. Every day after school we would meet and they would help not only with assembling the robot but also with planning, organizing events and introducing Instagram.

Training from experts

At the beginning of our journey, we focused on building a solid foundation. We started with additional lessons with physics teachers who helped us understand the key topics in mechanics and the physical principles that the robot would operate on. Then we made an open session with the professor from AITU - Darkhan Muratovich, who gave us information about coding, robotics, technological projects and basics in IT.

Meets with experienced teams

Also, we conducted 6 online meetings with other foreign robotics teams, where we exchanged experience, discussed technical solutions, and shared best practices. And other teams from Kazakhstan too

Meeting in NIS IB

On January 19 our team held an event at the NIS IB - we have visited this school to present our team, encourage students to join the robotics community and introduce them to the FIRST Tech Challenge. We explained how competitions are structured, what skills are essential to develop and how to start your own robotics team. We managed to engage students in STEM and grow the local robotics community

RISE IN STEM: GIRLS

Moreover, our team organized the RIZE IN STEM: GIRLS event on 20 January, to inspire female students to pursue STEM and technology. Speakers from Technovation Girls and the NU ACM-Woman Student Chapter shared their experiences and practical advice. The event helped students gain confidence and explore new opportunities in STEM, while teaching us event planning, communication and leadership skills.

Excursion for 6th graders

On Saturday, January 31st, we invited sixth-grade students to our school lab to introduce them to the robot development process. Our drivers and builders gave them a brief tour of our workspace and introduced our team. We demonstrated how we design, build, and test our robot, inspiring the students to become interested in robotics and explore the world of engineering and innovation.
